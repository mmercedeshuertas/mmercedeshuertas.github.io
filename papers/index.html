ICT International Doctoral School | Trento, Italy | 2015 - 2019

Coordinator of a project that involved primary schools teachers who wanted to improve the organisation of teaching materials, moving from an analogical solution to a digital one.

  • Earned teachers’ trust, understand their needs and expectations, and involve them in co-design activities to create a solution together. To do that, I involved, coordinated and managed a group of sixteen participants.
  • Conducted benchmark research, planned and organised activities, prioritising some over others. Analysed qualitative data to propose design activities and design trends:
  • We successfully created an online repository to organise materials facilitating storing, sharing and reusing tasks. Moreover, we established a protocol to organise materials by subject and course.
  • Wrote and participated in scientific publications that I presented to different audiences.  

KAIST (Korea Advanced Institure for Science and Technology) | Daejeon, South Korea | 2018

Awarded with a personal grant (10.000€) from Sorbonne Univeristè to collaborate with Juho Kim (PhD. by MIT).

  • Participated in the design of the questions of the interviews with participants for the Ministry of Finance of South Korea, the non-disclosure agreement considering South Korean privacy law.
  • Collaborated in the design of the subsequent focus group.
  • Conducted a round of usability testing of a Mobile App remotely through Skype.

University of Trento | Trento, Italy | 2014 - 2017

Awarded with a Marie Curie Early Stage Researcher (ESR) within the ESSENCE-network. Responsible of conducting usability testing over a user nterface (UI) to collect users’ contributions.

  • Contacted, involved, and managed remotely a group of thirteen participants.  
  • Conducted benchmark research on existing online contribution systems as well as the design and facilitation of usability testing selecting the most appropriate methods.
  • Analysed qualitative and quantitative data, proposing a new design direction that was reflected in the wireframes and, later, in the new UI.
  • The new design of the UI resulted in an increase of 46% in the number of contributions which impacted the quality of the resource as well as in the associated services provided, promoting the participation of the group in research projects.
  • I established a trust relationship with the participants through a friendly approach and constant communication that influenced their engagement and contribution rate through the UI.
  • Wrote project reports, grant proposals, and scientific publications that were presented to different audiences in internal meetings and public events.
